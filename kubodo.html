Kubudo

Weapons: Includes traditional Okinawan weapons such as the bo staff, sai, tonfa, kama, and nunchaku, each with its own unique techniques and applications. Training: Involves kata (forms), partner drills, and sparring, focusing on weapon handling, defense, and disarmament techniques. Historical Significance: Kobudo developed as a means for Okinawan peasants to defend themselves against invaders, utilizing everyday farming tools as weapons. Modern Practice: Kobudo is practiced worldwide, with schools preserving and teaching traditional techniques and forms passed down through generations.
